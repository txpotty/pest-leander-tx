Common Squirrel Problems and the Need for Professional Squirrel Removal

Squirrels, while often viewed as cute backyard critters, can quickly become a significant nuisance and cause substantial damage to your Leander home. Understanding the common issues they create is the first step in recognizing the need for professional squirrel removal in Leander, TX. These problems go beyond simply seeing a squirrel in your yard; they involve intrusion, damage, and potential health risks that require expert attention. Ignoring these signs can lead to more significant and costly repairs down the line. Leander Pest Pros is equipped to handle these challenges effectively and efficiently.

Scratching Noises in Your Walls or Attic

One of the most common indicators of a squirrel infestation is scratching or scurrying noises coming from inside your walls, attic, or ceiling. Squirrels are active throughout the day, and these noises are often most noticeable in the early morning and late afternoon as they move in and out of their nesting areas. These sounds can be unnerving and are a clear sign that you have unwanted guests. The sounds might vary depending on how many squirrels are present and what they are doing – gnawing, building nests, or simply moving around. This audio evidence points to an established presence within your home's structure, which is why a professional inspection is paramount. Our team is skilled at identifying the source of these noises and confirming a squirrel presence within confined spaces. This is different from sounds you might hear from mice or rats, which are typically active at night. The diurnal nature of squirrels makes their activity during the day a key identifier.

Visible Damage to Your Property's Exterior

Squirrels possess strong teeth that grow continuously, leading them to constantly gnaw. This gnawing instinct is a primary cause of damage to your home's exterior. They might chew on fascia boards, soffits, siding, and trim to gain access to your attic or other enclosed spaces. They can also damage roof shingles and even create holes in your roofline, offering easy entry points. This damage not only compromises the structural integrity of your home but also leaves it vulnerable to other pests and water damage. The appearance of chewed wood, shredded materials, or even visible holes where there shouldn't be any is a strong indicator of squirrel activity. These signs are often visible from the ground and can be further investigated by our technicians. We take a closer look at eaves, vents, and roof intersections where squirrels often attempt to enter properties in the Leander area. This type of exterior damage is a clear signal that professional intervention for squirrel removal is necessary before the problems escalate.

Chewed Electrical Wires and Insulation

Once inside your home, squirrels can inflict even more serious damage. They are notorious for chewing on electrical wires, posing a significant fire hazard. Their gnawing instinct extends to insulation, which they use to build nests. Damaged insulation can lead to reduced energy efficiency and higher utility bills. This type of internal damage is often hidden from view, making it harder to detect until a problem arises. The risk of fire from chewed electrical wires is a serious concern and should not be ignored. If you suspect squirrel activity in your attic or walls, it's crucial to have it professionally inspected to assess for this dangerous damage. Our technicians are trained to look for signs of chewed wiring and damaged insulation during their interior inspection. This internal investigation is a critical part of our rodent control services, extending beyond just squirrel removal to encompass all potential rodent-related issues. We can also help with wildlife control that impacts your home's internal structure.

Squirrels Nesting in Your Chimney or Vents

Chimneys and vents provide warm, sheltered spaces that are attractive nesting sites for squirrels. A squirrel nest in your chimney can obstruct airflow and create a fire hazard. Nests in vents can block ventilation and potentially lead to moisture buildup and mold growth. The presence of nesting materials, such as leaves, twigs, and insulation, around or inside these openings is a strong indicator of squirrel nesting. Squirrels may also use these locations to have their young, further complicating the removal process. Removing nests from chimneys and vents requires specialized tools and expertise to ensure safety and prevent damage. Our team has the necessary equipment and training to safely remove squirrel nests from these challenging locations. We can help prevent future nesting by securing these entry points. This is a specialized service that goes beyond general residential pest control and requires the expertise of wildlife specialists. We also offer preventative solutions to secure these often-overlooked entry points.
